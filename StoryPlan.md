1. Working Title and Subtitle  
The Adventures of Luna and Max  

2. Target Age Range and Reading Level  
Ages 5-8, suitable for early elementary children developing reading skills.  

3. Ideal Audience  
Young children who enjoy adventure and mystery stories, along with parents, teachers, and caregivers looking for books that encourage teamwork and friendship values.  

4. Genre, Format and Estimated Length  
Genre: Adventure and Mystery  
Format: Illustrated picture book or early chapter book  
Estimated Length: Approximately 32-40 pages or 1500-2500 words  

5. Core Theme and Central Question  
Theme: Friendship and teamwork  
Central Question: How can working together help solve challenges and create new friendships?  

6. Main Message / Moral in One Sentence  
Working together helps overcome challenges and build meaningful friendships.  

7. Short Synopsis  
Luna, an adventurous young fox, notices that the stars are disappearing from their forest night sky. Determined to solve this mystery, she teams up with Max, a clever and resourceful raccoon. Together, they follow clues, meet various forest friends, and face obstacles. Their journey leads them to Shadow, a mysterious and misunderstood figure who has been causing the stars to vanish because he feels lonely. Instead of confrontation, Luna and Max show kindness by inviting Shadow to join their circle of friends. With teamwork and newfound friendship, they restore the stars, bringing light and happiness back to their forest home.  

8. Main Characters  
- Luna (Protagonist): Adventurous, curious, brave; struggles with uncertainty but is motivated to find answers.  
- Max (Sidekick): Clever, resourceful, loyal; faces challenges in trusting others fully.  
- Bella (Friend): Wise, caring, supportive; helps guide Luna and Max with encouragement and advice.  
- Shadow (Antagonist): Mysterious, tricky, misunderstood; acts out of loneliness and is uncertain about friendship.  

9. Setting and Atmosphere  
The story is set in a vibrant, magical forest where the natural world feels alive and full of wonder. The time is present-day or timeless, with a gentle mystical tone created by the disappearing stars. The atmosphere blends curiosity, adventure, and warmth, culminating in a joyful sense of community and belonging.  

10. Emotional and Educational Goals  
Children should feel intrigued, inspired, and comforted by the story. Emotionally, it fosters empathy and kindness towards others who may seem different or troubled. Educationally, it highlights the importance and power of teamwork, communication, problem-solving, and inclusion.
