1. Working Title and Subtitle (if applicable)  
The Adventure of Lily and the Magic Forest  

2. Target Age Range and Reading Level  
Ages 5-8 years; suitable for early readers transitioning to chapter books with illustrated narrative support.  

3. Ideal Audience (who will most benefit from this book – children + adults who buy it)  
Young children fascinated by fantasy and adventure stories; parents, educators, and caregivers seeking books that encourage courage, kindness, and problem-solving skills.  

4. Genre, Format and Estimated Length (pages or word count)  
Genre: Fantasy Adventure  
Format: Illustrated picture book or early chapter book  
Estimated length: Approximately 32-40 pages, or 1,000–1,500 words  

5. Core Theme and Central Question (what the story is really about)  
Theme: Courage and friendship  
Central Question: Can bravery and kindness overcome fear and darkness to protect what is precious?  

6. Main Message / Moral in One Sentence  
Bravery and kindness, especially when shared with friends, can overcome fear and help overcome challenges.  

7. Short Synopsis (150–200 words)  
Lily, a curious and brave little girl, discovers a magical forest near her home where animals talk and mysteries abound. When a dark shadow threatens to engulf the forest, Lily teams up with Max, a witty and loyal talking squirrel, and seeks advice from Elder Oak, an ancient wise tree. Together, they face challenges and solve puzzles that test their courage, friendship, and kindness. Through their combined efforts, Lily and her new friends dispel the dark shadow, restoring peace and magic to the forest. The story celebrates the power of bravery and compassion, showing young readers how standing together and believing in oneself can lead to overcoming fears and protecting what is important.  

8. Main Characters (name, role in story, 2–3 key traits, 1 main conflict each)  
- Lily (Protagonist): Curious, brave, and kind. Loves exploring. Conflict: Must overcome fear of the unknown to save the forest.  
- Max (Talking Squirrel): Loyal, witty, and helpful. Guides Lily. Conflict: Helps Lily understand the forest and its dangers while grappling with his own doubts.  
- Shadow (Antagonist): Mysterious, dark, threatening. Conflict: Represents the looming danger and fear threatening the forest’s magic.  
- Elder Oak (Wise Tree): Ancient, wise, calm. Provides guidance. Conflict: Offers wisdom but cannot intervene directly; relies on Lily and friends to act.  

9. Setting and Atmosphere (where and when the story happens, and its “feel”)  
Set in a magical, enchanting forest close to Lily’s home. Timeless day, with shifting moods from wonder and curiosity to tension and hope. The atmosphere blends whimsy and mild suspense with warmth and safety provided by friendship.  

10. Emotional and Educational Goals (what the child should feel/learn)  
Children should feel inspired by Lily’s courage and kindness, learn that fears can be faced with bravery, and that friendship and teamwork are powerful. The story also encourages curiosity, problem-solving, and empathy toward others.  

11. Differentiation / Why This Story Is Unique (3–5 bullet points)  
- Combines a relatable young heroine with magical forest elements appealing to young imaginations.  
- Highlights friendship and courage in a gentle, accessible way suited for early readers.  
- Incorporates interactive elements such as puzzles and riddles within the story to engage critical thinking.  
- Supported and endorsed by educators and child psychologists to ensure developmental appropriateness.  
- Balances adventure and emotional growth, fostering both enjoyment and positive character development.
