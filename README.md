# Title
The Frog Who Wanted to Sing

## Logline / Short Description
Freddy, a small frog who adores music but is hilariously off-key, decides to enter the Forest Singing Contest. With encouragement from friends and a choice to sing from the heart, he discovers that perseverance and being true to yourself can win connection and pride — even if the tune isn't perfect.

## Theme and Moral
- Themes: Perseverance; empathy and encouragement (original shorthand: "P, e").
- Moral: Perseverance and authenticity matter more than perfect performance — your unique voice has value.

## Target Age & Genre
- Target age: 6–8 years
- Genre: Humorous animal fable / Picture book

## Story Summary

### Beginning
Freddy the frog loves melodies and dreams of performing in the annual Forest Singing Contest. Though he practices by the pond, his croaks are cheerfully off-key. Encouraged by his friends Rosie and Luna, Freddy decides to enter despite nervousness and teasing from a few peers.

### Main Conflict
Freddy wrestles with self-doubt and outside criticism. Other performers are smooth and practiced, and Mr. Owl — the contest judge — seems to favor polished technique. Freddy tries to imitate others' styles but still sounds different. He must decide whether to give up, hide, or perform in his own voice.

### Resolution
Freddy chooses to perform at the contest's open-mic and sings with wholehearted enthusiasm rather than technical perfection. His unique croak-song — backed by rhythm (noted in the original structure as "Gracie’s rhythm") — gradually wins the audience's smiles and applause. Mr. Owl acknowledges Freddy's bravery and invites him to sing again. Freddy learns that persistence and authenticity bring pride and connection; he gains confidence and a place in the forest community.

## Main Characters
- Freddy (Main protagonist) — A small, earnest green frog who adores music but is decidedly tone-deaf. Determined, optimistic, and vulnerable; Freddy learns to keep trying and to take pride in his own sound.
- Luna (Mentor / Friend) — A gentle, wise firefly who offers encouragement and practical practice tips. Calm and compassionate, she helps Freddy find confidence and perspective.
- Rosie (Ally / Friend) — A cheerful rabbit who loves to clap and dance to music. Enthusiastic and supportive, she rehearses with Freddy and celebrates his progress.
- Mr. Owl (Judge / Obstacle) — The respected, somewhat stern owl who judges the singing contest. He initially values polished technique but ultimately recognizes courage and heart.
